Demographic data:

I chose to focus on the demographic variables: population density, life expectancy and income in dollars. High life expectancy can affect the rate because the coronavirus disease affect more the elderly. High population density can affect the rate because there is higher possibility to get infected in dense areas. Low income can influence the rate because maybe the sanitation is bad in low-income areas. Some of the countries dont have al least one of this variables so I dropped them (in the end, we have 179 observations in the dataset).

Prediction model:

Firstly I split the data to a training set, which contains 70% of the data, and test set the contains the remained 30%.

Secondly, I chose to use random forest model for predicting the death rate.
The hyperparameter in random forest is how many variables the algorithm will use.
I chose to give it a range of parameters amount from 2 to 5 in order to find the optimized parameters amount (the chosen amount is 1).
Also I used reapeted 10-fold cross validation for hyperparameter optimization.
Moreover I decided to keep the number of trees in the random forest on default (500) for better prediction estimation.

The following table compare the train and test errors with two different measures (RMSE and MAE).

All the error are low, so we succeeded to predict the rate. But the low number of variables and observations might affect our predictions. Future researches should consider more variables and find more reliable demographic datasets.
